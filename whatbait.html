    What Lure Should you Use?

    Fake Lures

    Fake bait means plastic bait. These include things like divers, jigs, and plastic worms. These baits are common and easy to get. You can find them at your local fishing store. However, they can sometimes cost a lot, especially if you start amassing your collection. These plastic baits each have a gimmick. Some have tails that wiggle, some have beaks that help it dive, and some shine. But let's just talk about divers for now.

    Divers are lures with a clear beak on the head of it. These beaks are essential to the functionality of these special lures. The longer the beak the deeper the lure dives. This is because when you reel in the lure, the water run against the lure and pushes it down. The quicker your reel, the deeper it goes because of the beak. This allows you to catch fish in deeper waters. Depending on the water depth and what you want to catch, you will have to use different lengths of beaks on your lures.

    Live Lures

    Live lures are in the name, these are live worms, insects, and even smaller fish that you use to catch fish with. While most live lures are cheap, such as worms and crickets, they might take a little effort to collect. Some live lures are expensive especially if they're specialized or rare. However, most fishers have little need for these expensive live lures.

    These lures are effective because they move and struggle underwater. Movement and color are important aspects in attracting fish, as well as smell. Although it might not be as apparent above water, fish can notice the smells and of live bait underwater. Certain fish may be more suceptive to it, but every little bit helps your chances.
